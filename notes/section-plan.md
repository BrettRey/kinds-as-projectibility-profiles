# Section Plan: Kinds as Projectibility Profiles

## Review Decisions

Roughdraft review approved the overall architecture and asked for several
tightenings before drafting. I will treat these as controlling decisions:

- A projectibility profile is an analytic specification of a worldly pattern,
  not a merely conventional representation and not the worldly pattern by
  itself. A kind claim succeeds when the specified profile corresponds to a
  real pattern that licenses the proposed projections.
- "Projectibility first" is a methodological and explanatory order, not a claim
  that projectibility is metaphysically prior to every stabilizing structure.
  Start by specifying the inference; then ask what worldly relations, if any,
  make it reliable.
- Scope conditions belong in the core package. Fields, practices, institutions,
  and purposes are optional kinds of scope condition, not part of every kind.
- The four-role taxonomy is nested: controllers are a demanding subtype of
  maintainers; maintainers are a subtype of stabilizers; stabilizers support
  profiles. Profiles themselves are the target structure being specified.
- Section 4 should demonstrate portability without creating new evidence
  burdens. Deitality can be the main concrete example; typology and
  truth-tracking should be brief pointers.
- Section 5 should be foregrounded as the paper's disciplinary payoff:
  projectibility-profile talk is useful only if it tells us when to demote,
  localize, or reject a kind claim.

## Aim

Write a short synthetic position paper that makes the mature vocabulary citable:
kinds are projectibility profiles, where a profile is a structured pattern of
properties, diagnostics, stabilizers, limits, and failure modes that licenses
specified inferences under specified conditions. Homeostasis is one possible
stabilizing pattern, not the master criterion.

The paper should not repeat the HPC book or the full argument of "Not every
stable cluster is homeostatic." Its job is to provide a clean portable
formulation that other papers can cite when they need the profile vocabulary.

   - Falsification condition: if the draft needs detailed case studies to be

2. "Projectibility profile" is the headline concept.
   - Falsification condition: if the central sections spend more space on

3. The paper can cite internal portfolio results without depending on them.
   - Falsification condition: if a reader needs to accept deitality,

4. The paper should preserve level discipline.
   - Falsification condition: if profiles, mechanisms, fields, practices,
     categories, and judgments are allowed to explain one another without level
     markers, the vocabulary becomes too loose to be useful.

## Proposed Thesis

Kinds are projectibility profiles: structured patterns of properties,
diagnostics, stabilizers, limits, and failure modes that license specified
inferences under specified conditions. The first question is not "what mechanism
holds this cluster together?" but "what does this category let us project, under
which contrasts, and with what limits?" Stabilizing mechanisms matter because
they explain why those projections work. Homeostatic control is a demanding
subcase, not the default form of kindhood.

Two clarifications should appear early. First, this is not Goodman's
entrenchment account of projectibility. The paper uses "projectible" in a
Boydian realist sense: the predicate or category earns projection because the
world supplies relevant order, not merely because a predicate has a successful
history in our practice. Second, purposes affect which available projections
matter for inquiry; they do not create the availability of those projections.

## Proposed Section Architecture

### 1. Introduction: Kinds After Homeostasis

Open with the recurring problem: anti-essentialist kind theory needed a way to
make non-essential categories real and projectible, but "homeostatic" has become
too broad. The paper's proposal is not to discard Boyd's insight, but to put the
projection question first and treat mechanisms as supports for profiles.

Deliverables:

- State the thesis in one paragraph.
- Say explicitly that this is a synthesis and vocabulary paper.
- Preview the profile package: projection target, stable profile, ordering
  relations, stabilizers, failure modes, and scope conditions.

### 2. The Profile Package

Define a projectibility profile as a structured answer to six questions:

1. What is the projection target?
2. What observable profile supports it?
3. What relations make the profile non-accidental?
4. What stabilizes the profile across relevant contexts or timescales?
5. What failure modes mark the profile's limits?
6. Under which scope conditions is the profile projectible?

Pin the nested roles in one compact paragraph:

- A profile is the structured pattern whose partial observation licenses
  specified inferences.
- A stabilizer is any relation, constraint, mechanism, or practice that helps
  make that projection reliable.
- A maintainer is a dynamic stabilizer that preserves or regenerates the profile
  over time or under perturbation.
- A controller is a maintainer with perturbation-sensitive organization that
  preserves a higher-scale relation through lower-scale variation.

Reserve "homeostatic" for the controller case.

### 3. Why Projectibility Comes First

Make the core philosophical move as a methodological/explanatory priority:
mechanisms do not make a category useful by themselves. They matter because they
explain why observing some parts of a profile licenses expectations about
others. The projection target tells us what evidence to seek, but the evidence
must still be supplied by worldly order.

Use compact contrasts:

- A recurring cluster with no specified inference is descriptive, not yet a kind
  claim.
- A stable profile can justify inquiry before its maintainer is known, though
  the reliability of the projection still depends on whatever worldly structure
  makes the profile non-accidental.
- A maintained profile can fail to be homeostatic if it lacks corrective
  control.
- A category can be real for one set of scope conditions and thin for another.

This section is the place for Boyd, Slater, Khalidi, and Goodman, using already
verified bibliography keys where possible.

### 4. Profiles Across Levels

Generalize the apparatus beyond biological natural kinds without making three
new case arguments. Profiles can be behavioral, algorithmic,
corpus-distributional, model-internal, institutional, normative, or
social-practical, but the explanatory level has to be marked because different
evidence licenses different projections.

Use deitality as the compact worked demonstration: its value lies in predictions
across diagnostics, mismatch cases, manipulation, variation, and acquisition.
Then give one-sentence pointers to typological comparanda and truth-tracking
profiles as places where the same vocabulary already does useful work.

State the field/practice guardrail here: fields, practices, and institutions can
be scope conditions or stabilizing backgrounds; they should not be promoted to
kinds without a separate profile claim.

### 5. Failure Modes and Demotion

Foreground this section as the paper's main contribution after the thesis. A
profile view needs ways to say that a category is not projectible enough,
projectible only locally, or stabilized by the wrong kind of process for the
claim being made.

Core failure modes:

- Thin profile: diagnostics don't travel beyond the identifying cases.
- Wrong-level projection: a social-practical profile is treated as a
  model-internal or biological mechanism.
- Decorative mechanism: a mechanism is named but no projection is derived from
  it.
- Over-homeostatization: stability or maintenance is mislabeled as corrective
  control.
- Scope overreach: a profile projectible under one set of conditions is exported
  elsewhere without fresh tests.

This section should make the paper useful as a demotion checklist.

### 6. What the Vocabulary Buys

Close with the payoff:

- It preserves Boyd's anti-essentialist realism without requiring essences.
- It incorporates Slater's stability-first lesson without stopping at
  stability.
- It incorporates Khalidi's network discipline without making mechanisms the
  contribution.
- It lets linguistic, typological, social, AI, and normative categories be
  compared without forcing them into a biological homeostasis template.

The conclusion should be short and quotable: a category is a profile, stabilized
by mechanisms, projectible under specified conditions.

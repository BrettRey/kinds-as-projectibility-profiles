1. **One-Sentence Summary**

The paper argues that anti-essentialist kinds should be analysed as projectibility profiles: property-and-relation patterns that license specified inferences under specified conditions, with mechanisms, networks, and homeostasis treated as increasingly demanding ways of explaining why those projections hold.

2. **Strengths**

- The paper understands the central Boydian payoff well: projectibility is not decorative here. Sections 1 and 3 repeatedly make the right move: mechanisms matter because they explain projection, not because naming a mechanism automatically makes a kind real.

- The Q3/Q4 distinction in Section 2 is promising. Separating synchronic structural order from diachronic persistence gives the paper a useful way to avoid treating all clustered properties as homeostatic.

- The paper is unusually careful about demotion. Section 5 gives the framework teeth by distinguishing thin profiles, wrong-level projection, decorative mechanism, over-homeostatization, and scope overreach.

3. **Weaknesses**

- Section 2 blurs the paper’s own Q4/Q5 distinction when it defines a `stabilizer`. The fourth question asks why co-availability is non-accidental; the fifth asks what keeps or regenerates that co-availability over time. But the definition of `stabilizer` says it explains why source and target properties are co-available rather than merely correlated. That makes `stabilizer` look like structural ordering, not diachronic stabilization. I would either rename the Q4 relation or reserve `stabilizer` for the Q5 role.

- The extension beyond causal networks is underdisciplined. The paper says ordering may be functional, conventional, normative, algorithmic, or evidential, while also saying mere cue status is not enough. That is the right warning, but it needs a criterion. In Section 2, what distinguishes a genuine non-causal ordering relation from a reliable diagnostic or evidential proxy?

- Section 4 gestures at linguistic, typological, and truth-tracking cases, but it does not yet show one full network/profile analysis. From this seat, the paper needs one worked case where the projection target, ordered properties, diagnostics, causal or causal-normative support, diachronic stabilization, and failure conditions are all separated. Without that, the network language risks becoming a portable vocabulary rather than a disciplined explanatory structure.

4. **Key Q&A Question**

In one non-biological case, preferably a linguistic or social one, which relations in the profile are genuinely explanatory rather than diagnostic, which are causal or causal-historical, which are normative or conventional, and what projection would fail if that ordering relation were removed?

5. **Verdict**

**Revise & Resubmit.** The projectibility-first architecture is strong, but the paper needs a sharper account of non-causal ordering and a worked example that proves the network extension has causal discipline rather than just classificatory flexibility.

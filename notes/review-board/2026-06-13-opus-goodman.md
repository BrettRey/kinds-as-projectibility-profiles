**Reviewer seat:** Nelson Goodman — Projectibility Reviewer
*(A standpoint seat based on the public work on projectibility, induction, and entrenchment, and on the role that work plays in this paper. Not a claim about any actual person's private views.)*

---

### 1. One-sentence summary

A kind is a worldly property-and-relation profile whose partial observation licenses specified inferences under specified conditions, and "homeostatic" should be demoted from the default name for kindhood to a demanding subcase (corrective control) within a tiered vocabulary of stabilizer / maintainer / controller.

### 2. Strengths

- **The tiering is real work, not relabeling.** The separation of *stable* / *network-ordered* / *maintained* / *controlled* (Section 2, and the conclusion's "Some are stable… Only the last deserves the strict homeostatic label") is a genuine correction. The paper is right that a profile "can be stable without a known maintainer… ordered without being maintained… maintained without being controlled." That is a sharper inventory than most cluster-theoretic writing offers, and it is correctly motivated by the observation that "homeostatic" had been smuggled in as a synonym for recurrence.

- **Projectibility is kept as the payoff, not decoration.** I came in suspicious that "projectibility" would be ornamental. It mostly isn't. The six-question package leads with the projection target (Section 2, Q1: "recurrence is cheap"); the demotion machinery in Section 5 cashes out in lost or localized *predictions*, not in metaphysical re-description; and the conclusion's closing questions are inference questions. The "decorative mechanism" failure mode and its operational test (Section 3: "if removing the mechanism leaves the same predictions in place, the mechanism has not earned its role") is exactly the discipline I would demand of anyone who wants worldly structure to do epistemic work. I learned from, and agree with, the framing that a list of co-occurring properties "becomes theoretically interesting only when it licenses some further expectation."

- **An exit procedure.** Giving anti-essentialist realism explicit demotion rules (Section 5) is more than most HPC papers attempt. "The vocabulary is useful only if it can say no" is the right standard.

### 3. Weaknesses

- **The "Boydian rather than Goodmanian" move (Section 3) is not a difference of emphasis; it is declining the problem the word names.** The paper writes: "It doesn't treat projectibility primarily as entrenchment in our predicate-using history. It asks what worldly order makes a category a reliable guide to further expectation." But the new riddle's entire point is that *worldly order does not sort the licensed projection from the unlicensed one*. Grue and green fit every observed emerald equally well; both are equally "non-accidental" relative to the evidence. To say projectibility is "what worldly order makes reliable" is to help yourself, by fiat, to precisely the discrimination the riddle says you cannot read off the cases. **Actionable:** either argue that entrenchment can be dropped (and show how worldly order alone breaks the grue symmetry), or concede that projectibility has an irreducible practice-side dimension and rename what the paper actually delivers (e.g., "stability profile" or "inductive-support profile"). Borrowing the prestige of Goodman 1955 for the title while disowning the riddle in one sentence is not fair use of the source.

- **The fourth question relocates the riddle into the "stabilizer"; it does not answer it.** Section 2's Q4 ("what makes source and target properties co-available rather than accidental co-occurrences") and the `stabilizer` definition are offered as what separates a real profile from a mere correlation. But whether a "stabilizer" exists, and whether it makes properties "co-available," depends on the vocabulary you are allowed to state the mechanism in. You can give a grue-ified mechanism description; you can state a "selectional constraint" or "ecological regularity" in deviant predicates. The non-accidentality test presupposes an already-privileged inductive vocabulary — which is the entrenchment fact the paper set aside. So the machinery doesn't dissolve the riddle; it pushes it one level down, from the predicate to the stabilizer description, where it sits unaddressed. **Actionable:** add a paragraph in Section 3 or 5 acknowledging that "non-accidental co-availability" is vocabulary-relative, and say what fixes the admissible vocabulary.

- **None of the five failure modes can demote a well-behaved-but-unprojectible profile.** Section 5's modes are thin, wrong-level, decorative-mechanism, over-homeostatized, scope-overreach. The grue case is *none* of these: its diagnostics are perfect, its fit on observed cases is total, its profile is fully and non-thinly specifiable, and its "stabilizer" can be stated. It fails to project anyway. The paper's watch-item — explaining why some apparently well-behaved profiles still fail — is therefore not met. The framework can demote profiles that are *visibly* deficient; it has no resource for the case where deficiency is invisible to every test it lists. **Actionable:** either add a sixth failure mode keyed to vocabulary/entrenchment, or explicitly scope the paper's claim to "profiles whose stabilizer is statable in independently entrenched predicates" and say so up front.

### 4. Key question at Q&A

Take grue. It has a fully specified profile, perfect diagnostics, total fit on every examined emerald, and I can hand you a "stabilizer" stated in grue-vocabulary. Which of your five failure modes demotes it — and when you reach for the answer, isn't every demotion rule you invoke ("non-accidental," "co-available," "the right level") already helping itself to an entrenched vocabulary you haven't earned? If so, in what sense is your account *Boydian rather than Goodmanian*, rather than simply Goodmanian-with-the-hard-part-omitted?

### 5. Verdict

**Revise & Resubmit.** The tiering and the demotion discipline are a real contribution worth publishing, but the central term is undercharged against the very riddle it is named after; the paper must either break the grue symmetry with worldly order alone or own the entrenchment dimension it currently waves off in one sentence.

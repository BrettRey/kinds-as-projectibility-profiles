**Reviewer Seat: Melanie Mitchell - AI and Truth-Tracking End-User**

1. **One-Sentence Summary**

The paper argues that anti-essentialist kinds should be analyzed as projectibility profiles: patterns of properties and relations that license specified inferences under specified conditions, with mechanisms and stabilizers earning their role only by explaining those projections.

2. **Strengths**

- The paper gets the HPC priority right: projectibility is the epistemic payoff, not a decorative afterthought. Sections 2 and 3 repeatedly ask what a category lets us infer before asking what stabilizes it.
- The distinction between stabilizers, maintainers, and controllers is useful and prevents a common overreach: treating any robust cluster as homeostatic.
- The truth-tracking example in Section 4 makes a promising AI-relevant move by separating surface improvement from truth-tracking improvement, especially in the contrast between self-consistency, retrieval, tools, and error correction.

3. **Weaknesses**

- Section 4’s truth-tracking paragraph is too compressed to carry the portability claim. It says fluent coherence and truth-tracking can come apart, but it doesn’t instantiate the six-part profile package: projection target, diagnostics, support relation, stabilizer, scope, and failure mode. Add a worked micro-case showing exactly what an LLM answer lets us predict under retrieval, tool use, self-consistency, and correction.
- The paper needs a sharper distinction between “accuracy-improving intervention” and “truth-tracking stabilizer.” Retrieval, tools, self-consistency, and error correction are not interchangeable support routes. Self-consistency may stabilize coherence while worsening shared falsehood; retrieval may stabilize provenance without guaranteeing interpretation; tools may stabilize measurement only when the tool call is well-scoped.
- The conclusion cashes out the framework well in general terms, but not enough in AI-facing terms. It asks “what does this category let us infer?” but the truth-tracking case should say what the category lets readers predict, explain, or generalize about robustness, transfer, and failure. Right now the AI example gestures at that payoff rather than delivering it.

4. **Key Q&A Question**

In the truth-tracking case, what specific observable failure would show that an LLM’s fluent success is not truth-tracking but benchmark familiarity or brittle pattern matching, and how would your profile analysis demote or relevel the category?

5. **Verdict**

**Revise & Resubmit.** The core HPC architecture is strong and projectibility-first, but the AI truth-tracking portability test needs a worked profile with operational contrasts before it can support the paper’s broader claim.

**Richard Boyd - HPC Reviewer**

**1. One-Sentence Summary**

The paper argues that anti-essentialist kind theory should treat kinds as projectibility profiles: structured property-and-relation patterns that license specified inferences under specified conditions, with mechanisms and stabilizers explaining that projectibility rather than replacing it.

**2. Strengths**

- Section 3 gets the core Boydian priority right: projectibility is the epistemic payoff, and mechanisms matter because they explain why projection works. This is not a mechanisms-first HPC paper with projectibility tacked on.
- The stabilizer / maintainer / controller distinction in Section 2 is genuinely useful. It gives the paper a way to stop overpromoting every stable or ordered cluster into “homeostasis.”
- Section 5 gives the framework realist discipline. The demotion rules make clear that failed projection should localize, relevel, or reject a kind claim rather than merely redescribe it.

**3. Weaknesses**

- The restriction of `homeostatic` to controller cases is too quick. From a Boydian seat, this risks narrowing HPC beyond what made it powerful: a flexible realism about clustered properties sustained by causal or explanatory relations, not necessarily feedback-control architecture. Section 2 should say more explicitly whether this is a terminological proposal, an interpretation of Boyd, or a revision of Boyd.
- The programmatic/explanatory distinction is named but underused. Section 3 says the projection target states the programmatic role and the profile gives explanatory support, but the paper needs to show how that distinction prevents “projectibility profile” from becoming a methodological checklist.
- The examples in Section 4 are too compressed to carry the burden. A Boydian reviewer will want one worked case where the paper names the projection, shows what supports it, identifies the stabilizer, and shows what demotion would look like if the projection failed. Otherwise the framework looks cleaner than its applications.

**HPC Projectibility Check**

- Projectibility as epistemic payoff: yes, centrally. This is the paper’s strongest feature.
- Mechanisms explain projectibility rather than replace it: mostly yes, though “stabilizer” sometimes does too much work at once.
- Conclusion cashes out in prediction/explanation/generalization: yes in slogan form, but it should end with a more concrete sense of what a profile lets investigators predict, explain, or generalize.

**4. Key Q&A Question**

What exactly does “projectibility profile” add to Boyd’s own programmatic/explanatory picture of accommodated kind terms, and why should a Boydian accept your narrower use of `homeostatic` rather than treat it as an optional subtype within a broader HPC realism?

**5. Verdict**

**Revise & Resubmit.** The projectibility-first framing is strong and recognizably Boydian, but the paper needs a worked case and a more careful defense of its restrictive use of `homeostatic` before it can claim to preserve rather than replace HPC realism.

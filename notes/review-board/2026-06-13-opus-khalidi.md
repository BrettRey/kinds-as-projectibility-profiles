## Review — Muhammad Ali Khalidi seat (Network Reviewer)

*Standpoint seat based on the causal-network account of natural and human kinds. Not the named person, not a claim about his private views.*

### 1. One-sentence summary

Kinds are projectibility profiles, worldly property-and-relation patterns whose partial observation licenses specified inferences under specified conditions, with "homeostatic" demoted from the default form of kindhood to one demanding tier (controllers) inside a profile/stabilizer/maintainer/controller hierarchy.

### 2. Strengths

- **The Q4/Q5 split is a real contribution, and it is the network-discipline move I would most want made.** Separating "why are the projected-from and projected-to properties co-available" (synchronic structural order, Section 2 q4) from "what keeps the profile available across perturbation" (modal/diachronic stabilization, q5) cleanly disaggregates what "homeostatic" smears together. The line "Network order says why co-availability isn't coincidence; stabilization says why it persists" is exactly the distinction a causal-network account needs and rarely states.
- **The decorative-mechanism test does genuine work.** "If removing the mechanism leaves the same predictions in place, the mechanism has not earned its role in the kind claim" (Section 3, reused as failure mode 3) is the right operationalization of explanatory ordering among properties. It keeps mechanism subordinate to projection without making it idle, which is precisely the HPC payoff check: projectibility is the target, mechanisms explain it rather than replace it. On that score the paper is disciplined throughout.
- **It refuses to identify a cluster with a network.** "Kinds are not mere concatenations of properties, but nodes in ordered networks where some properties help explain others" (Section 2) reads my account correctly, and the over-homeostatization failure mode (Section 5) gives the framework an exit procedure, not just an on-ramp to realism.

### 3. Weaknesses

- **"Evidential" order (Section 2, q4) contradicts the paper's own "mere cue status isn't enough" constraint.** The fourth question lists the ordering relation as possibly "functional, conventional, normative, algorithmic, or evidential rather than narrowly causal," but two sentences later insists "mere cue status isn't enough." Evidential ordering *is* cue status. And Section 2 q3 already assigns diagnostics to the *specification*, not the profile. So "evidential order" is either redundant with diagnostics (and shouldn't appear in the list of worldly ordering relations) or it smuggles a fact-about-us into the profile. **Fix:** distinguish a worldly relation that happens to be evidential (a symptom causally downstream of a disease, which orders the network) from epistemic cue-tracking (which belongs to diagnostics), and drop "evidential" from the q4 list of ordering relations. As written, the paper risks the exact network-overuse it elsewhere warns against.
- **The "causal, causal-historical, or causal-normative support" clause is asserted once and never applied.** It appears in Section 2 as the price of the strong Khalidian kind claim, then disappears. The social-case profiles (institutional marks, sanctions, uptake, repair) are precisely where conventional order needs causal-normative backing to count as a kind rather than a stipulated regularity, and the paper never works one example through. **Fix:** one worked social or grammatical case showing conventional order *backed by* causal-normative support, and what distinguishes it from a merely conventional regularity that fails to be a kind. Without it, "the ordering relation still has to explain the declared projection" is a promissory note.
- **The Q4/Q5 split may not survive human kinds, where it is sold most cheaply.** The paper says "some answers to the fourth question also answer the fifth, but the claims differ." For biological cases, fine. But for many social and grammatical kinds the synchronic structural order is *non-accidental only because of its causal history* (diachronic source, transmission, sedimented convention). There the q4 order is partly constituted by q5 history; pulling them apart misdescribes the case. **Fix:** demonstrate a *human*-kind case where structural order and diachronic stabilization genuinely come apart, not only the biological illustration the split rides on.

### 4. Question for Q&A

You allow conventional, normative, and (in the q4 list) evidential ordering as non-causal extensions of network order, yet you also impose "mere cue status isn't enough" and require causal, causal-historical, or causal-normative backing for the strong kind claim. So for a social or grammatical category: once diagnostics are assigned to the specification and causal-normative support is conceded as the price of kindhood, is there any *genuinely non-causal* ordering relation left that still grounds a kind, or has the network just been reduced to a causal one with extra labels?

### 5. Verdict

**Revise & Resubmit.** A strong, network-responsible synthesis whose central distinctions (Q4/Q5, the decorative-mechanism test) earn their place, but the non-causal extensions are underspecified at exactly the points where causal discipline is most at risk, and one worked social case backing the causal-normative clause would settle most of it.

**Reviewer Seat: Nelson Goodman - Projectibility Reviewer**

1. **One-Sentence Summary**  
The paper argues that anti-essentialist kinds should be treated as projectibility profiles: property-and-relation patterns that license specified inferences under specified conditions, with mechanisms and stabilizers earning their role only by explaining why those projections work.

2. **Strengths**
- The paper keeps projectibility in the foreground more successfully than most HPC-style accounts: Sections 2 and 3 repeatedly insist that recurrence, clustering, stabilization, and mechanism talk matter only insofar as they license further expectation.
- The stabilizer / maintainer / controller distinction in Section 2 is useful and genuinely clarifies a common inflation in Boydian vocabulary, especially the slide from stable cluster to homeostasis.
- Section 5 gives the framework real bite by making demotion, localization, wrong-level projection, decorative mechanism, and over-homeostatization explicit. That is the paper’s strongest Goodmanian move.

3. **Weaknesses**
- The paper says its use is Boydian rather than Goodmanian, but it doesn’t yet earn the contrast. Section 3 rejects entrenchment as primary, but it needs to explain why “worldly order” is enough to distinguish genuinely projectible predicates from merely well-fitting, artificially constructed, or practice-dependent ones. Otherwise the Goodman problem is named but partly evaded.
- The central notion of “licenses specified inferences” needs sharper constraints. Section 2 lists prediction, explanation, classification, intervention, measurement, comparison, and learning, but these are not equally projective. The paper should say what counts as a successful projection and what kind of failure defeats kind status rather than merely narrows scope.
- The examples in Section 4 remain too programmatic. They say the profile “supports expectations,” but they don’t show a case where two profiles fit observed data and only one licenses future projection. A Goodmanian reviewer will want at least one worked contrast: same observed clustering, different projection status.

4. **Key Question For Q&A**  
What prevents a projectibility profile from becoming a refined checklist plus scope note: that is, what exactly distinguishes a profile that merely summarizes observed regularities from one that licenses projection to unobserved, counterfactual, or perturbed cases?

5. **Verdict: Revise & Resubmit**  
The paper has the right architecture and keeps projectibility as the epistemic payoff, but it needs a sharper answer to the Goodmanian challenge: why some well-behaved profiles still fail to project.

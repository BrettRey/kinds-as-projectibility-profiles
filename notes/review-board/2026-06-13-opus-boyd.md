# Review — Richard Boyd seat (HPC / scientific realism)

*Standpoint seat. This reconstructs how a Boydian realist would read the paper, grounded in the public HPC literature and the role that literature plays in the draft. Not a claim about any individual's private views.*

## 1. One-sentence summary

Kinds are "projectibility profiles" (worldly property-and-relation patterns whose partial observation licenses specified inferences under stated conditions), and "homeostatic" should be reserved for one demanding subcase, corrective control, rather than naming kindhood as such.

## 2. Strengths

- **The disaggregation is real and broadly faithful to accommodation.** Splitting the overloaded "homeostatic" into profile / stabilizer / maintainer / controller (Section 2) is a genuine contribution. It recovers what the accommodation thesis was always about: classificatory practice tracking non-accidental worldly structure. The "stabilizers needn't be active processes" line correctly resists collapsing all kinds into biological maintenance.
- **The decorative-mechanism test has teeth.** Section 3's rule ("if removing the mechanism leaves the same predictions in place, the mechanism hasn't earned its role"), reused as a demotion rule in Section 5, is a sharp, usable discipline. It is exactly the kind of constraint that keeps mechanism-talk honest without inflating it.
- **An explicit exit procedure.** Section 5's failure modes (thin profile, wrong-level projection, decorative mechanism, over-homeostatization, scope overreach) answer the standing charge that anti-essentialist accounts are too permissive. "Demotion belongs inside realism" is the right instinct.

## 3. Weaknesses (actionable)

- **Realism risks being thinned to methodology, and a footnote is hiding the decision.** The paper twice calls its contribution "regulative rather than metaphysical in the grand style" and gives existing resources "an order of use." But the Boydian claim is metaphysical-cum-epistemic: the kind is *real* because real worldly relations sustain the cluster; projectibility is the epistemic *symptom* of that structure, not the truthmaker. Section 3 makes projectibility "the target of the kind claim" and mechanisms merely "explain why projection works," which inverts the priority, then the footnote disclaims "the stronger constitutive thesis." That footnote is doing load the text won't carry: it ducks the one question a realist most needs answered. **Action:** state plainly whether the worldly profile or the licensed inference is the truthmaker. If it's the profile, say how the view differs from accommodationist realism other than in vocabulary; if it's the inference, explain how you avoid the pragmatist reading you elsewhere refuse.
- **The narrowed "homeostatic" is too restrictive and reads the metaphor literally.** Reserving "homeostatic" for perturbation-sensitive controllers (Section 2, citing `reynolds2026notEveryStableCluster`) treats the cybernetic image as the criterion and then polices the broader literature for looseness. The original "homeostatic mechanisms" usage was deliberately broad and included relations among the properties themselves, not only corrective-control loops. As written, the paper risks winning a terminological fight while conceding the substance. **Action:** either defend the narrow reading exegetically against the Boyd texts, or rename the strict tier (e.g., "control kinds") and stop annexing "homeostatic." *(Source-grounding flag: my characterization of Boyd's broad usage should be checked against `boyd1991`/`boyd1999` before the paper relies on it; I have not re-read those here, and the strict definition is asserted via `reynolds2026notEveryStableCluster`, which I have also not read.)*
- **The programmatic/explanatory distinction is invoked but underpowered.** It appears once (Section 3: projection target = programmatic role, profile = candidate explanatory support) and is then left to coast. But this distinction is where the real danger lives: a programmatic definition can stall and never get causally cashed out, and the paper's "name the projection first" ordering *is* the programmatic stage. The draft never shows the distinction doing work that plain "target vs. support" doesn't. **Action:** give one worked case sitting at the programmatic stage, say what evidence would move it to explanatory, and show what the profile vocabulary adds over the original two-stage account.

## 4. Key question at Q&A

On your view, what makes a kind claim *true*, the worldly profile or the inferences it licenses? If the profile, how is this more than accommodationist realism relabeled? If the inferences, how do you keep "projectibility profile" from collapsing into the instrumentalism your demotion-inside-realism section seems to reject?

## 5. Verdict

**Revise & Resubmit.** The disaggregation is valuable and largely faithful in spirit, but the paper must decide on the page whether it preserves causal realism or substitutes a methodology, and must justify rather than stipulate its narrowing of "homeostatic."

---

On the editor's HPC checks: projectibility *is* foregrounded as the payoff, and mechanisms are explicitly cast as explaining projection rather than replacing it (this is the paper's best feature). The conclusion does cash out in predict/explain/generalize questions. My disagreement is not that these checks fail, it's that passing them *methodologically* is being allowed to stand in for the metaphysical commitment that made HPC realism worth defending. I would not smooth that to consensus.

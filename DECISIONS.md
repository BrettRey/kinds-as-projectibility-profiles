# DECISIONS

2026-06-13 — Working title: "Kinds as Projectibility Profiles." Reason: gives the synthesis paper a mature-view label and avoids making homeostasis the headline criterion.

2026-06-13 — Scope: short synthetic position paper rather than another full theory paper. Reason: the contribution should be framework consolidation and citable vocabulary, not a repetition of the HPC book.

2026-06-13 — Source architecture: treat "Not every stable cluster is homeostatic" as the general correction, "Definiteness and Deitality in English" as the linguistic application, "Naturalizing Typological Kinds" as the methodological apparatus, and "Truth-Tracking Profiles" as the bridge from older HPC vocabulary to mature profile language.

2026-06-13 — Infrastructure: point the project to the central bibliography and central house style by symlink. Reason: this project should share the current house-style and central-bib surfaces rather than carrying stale local copies.

2026-06-13 — First-draft architecture: use a six-section compact synthesis structure: introduction, profile package, projectibility-first priority, cross-level portability, failure modes/demotion, and payoff. Reason: Roughdraft review confirmed that Sections 1–3 should define the view, Section 4 should show portability without new evidence burdens, Section 5 should provide the disciplinary teeth, and Section 6 should state the payoff.

2026-06-13 — Interpret "projectibility first" as a methodological and explanatory order, not a strong metaphysical grounding thesis. Reason: a short vocabulary paper can defend starting with the projection question, but a constitutive metaphysical thesis about what grounds projectibility would require a larger argument.

2026-06-13 — Treat a projectibility profile as an analytic specification of a worldly pattern. Reason: this avoids a slide between profile-as-representation and profile-as-worldly-structure while preserving the realist constraint that successful kind claims answer to available projections.

2026-06-13 — Use a nested role taxonomy: controllers are maintainers, maintainers are stabilizers, and stabilizers support profiles. Reason: the claim that homeostasis is a demanding subcase is a containment claim, not a loose family-resemblance claim.

2026-06-13 — Add a local verified deitality bibliography entry under a new key, rather than editing the central bibliography. Reason: the central entry uses an older subtitle, while the current local/public paper title is "Definiteness and Deitality in English: A Projectibility-First Account"; central-bib edits should be handled deliberately later.
